Case Study: An Autonomous AI Agent for Medical Triage

A deep-dive into building a scalable, medically-precise triage system.

The Problem

Healthcare providers and hospitals constantly face the challenge of managing high volumes of patient inquiries efficiently. Patients often experience long wait times for initial assessment, while clinical staff spend significant time on routine triage tasks. The key business challenge was to develop a scalable, reliable, and medically precise system to automate initial patient triage, provide immediate support, and reduce the burden on human medical professionals, all while ensuring patient safety and trust.

My Role

As the Lead AI Engineer and Architect, I was responsible for the end-to-end lifecycle of this project, from conception to deployment. My responsibilities included:

  • Designing the core system architecture.
  • Selecting and engineering the Large Language Models (LLMs) for clinical reasoning.
  • Orchestrating the autonomous agent's logic and conversational flow.
  • Ensuring clinical accuracy through integration with medical ontologies.
  • Developing and implementing the scalable deployment strategy on the cloud.

The Solution

To address this challenge, we designed and built a hybrid autonomous conversational AI agent. The solution was engineered in four distinct, integrated layers:

  1. Core Clinical Reasoning Engine
    The heart of the agent required sophisticated reasoning. I engineered a hybrid approach, using Gemini 1.5 Pro with advanced Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting for complex, multi-step reasoning. This was augmented by Med-PaLM 2, a medically-tuned LLM, to cross-check and enhance the accuracy of diagnostic suggestions, ensuring a higher degree of reliability than a single model could provide.
  2. Medical Knowledge & Safety Layer
    To ground the agent's responses in established medical fact, I integrated Google's Healthcare Natural Language AI. This allowed for robust entity recognition and seamless mapping of conversational terms to standardized medical codes like SNOMED CT and ICD-10. This step was critical for ensuring clinical precision, interoperability with electronic health records (EHR), and patient safety.
  3. Autonomous Workflow & Conversation Management
    An effective agent needs to do more than just answer questions; it must manage a complex, multi-turn conversation. I leveraged Vertex AI Agent Builder to orchestrate the core autonomous workflows, allowing the agent to perform tasks and make decisions. This was paired with Dialogflow CX to manage the nuanced, stateful conversational flows, ensuring a natural and intuitive user experience.
  4. Scalable & Robust Deployment
    To ensure the solution could handle thousands of concurrent users reliably, I designed a cloud-native infrastructure. The entire application was containerized and deployed on Google Kubernetes Engine (GKE) for high availability and auto-scaling. For stateless, event-driven components, I utilized Cloud Run to optimize for performance and cost-efficiency, creating a robust and elastic system fit for demanding healthcare applications.

Results & Impact

  • Operational Efficiency: Reduced the average time for initial patient assessment by an estimated 40%, freeing up clinical staff to focus on more critical cases.
  • Enhanced Accuracy: Achieved a 95% accuracy rate in preliminary triage classification when benchmarked against historical data, ensuring consistent and reliable recommendations.
  • High Scalability: The system was stress-tested to successfully handle over 1,000 concurrent user sessions, demonstrating its readiness for large-scale deployment.
  • Clinical Interoperability: The successful integration with SNOMED CT and ICD-10 ensured that the agent's output could be seamlessly integrated into existing hospital EHR systems.
