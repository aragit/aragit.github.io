Case Study: An Autonomous AI Agent for Medical Triage

A deep-dive into building a scalable, medically-precise triage system.

The Problem

Healthcare providers and hospitals constantly face the challenge of managing high volumes of patient inquiries efficiently. Patients often experience long wait times for initial assessment, while clinical staff spend significant time on routine triage tasks. The key business challenge was to develop a scalable, reliable, and medically precise system to automate initial patient triage, provide immediate support, and reduce the burden on human medical professionals, all while ensuring patient safety and trust.

My Role

As the Lead AI Engineer and Architect, I was responsible for the end-to-end lifecycle of this project, from conception to deployment. My responsibilities included:

  • Designing the core system architecture.
  • Selecting and engineering the Large Language Models (LLMs) for clinical reasoning.
  • Orchestrating the autonomous agent's logic and conversational flow.
  • Ensuring clinical accuracy through integration with medical ontologies.
  • Developing and implementing the scalable deployment strategy on the cloud.

Agent Architecture & Functionality

The agent was engineered to mimic a clinician’s stepwise reasoning process. Below is a concise walk-through of how it supports triage, diagnosis, and treatment planning.

  1. Symptom Intake & Triage
    • Dialogflow CX initiates a structured, multi-turn conversation to collect symptoms, vitals, and history.
    • Healthcare Natural Language AI maps every free-text symptom or drug to SNOMED-CT / ICD-10 codes for precision and interoperability.
    • Gemini 1.5 Pro (via Vertex AI Model Garden) applies chain-of-thought prompting to rank urgency, retrieving triage guidelines from the knowledge base in real time.
  2. Diagnosis Support
    • Med-PaLM 2 augments Gemini 1.5 Pro with specialized medical knowledge, generating differential diagnoses ranked by probability.
    • The drug-knowledge-graph API automatically screens for medication conflicts based on the patient’s current prescriptions.
  3. Treatment Planning
    • Vertex AI Agent Builder orchestrates guideline retrieval, conflict detection, and treatment suggestions in a single cohesive flow.
    • Recommendations are evidence-based, personalised to the patient’s profile, and presented for clinician review.
  4. Scalability & Safety
    • GPU-accelerated A2 VMs inside Google Kubernetes Engine handle inference bursts (tested to 1 000+ RPM).
    • Cloud Run manages stateless intake components; Cloud Load Balancer provides seamless autoscaling and zero-downtime roll-outs.

Clinical Scenario Snapshot

A patient reports “sharp chest pain, shortness of breath on exertion, and fatigue for five days”.

  1. The agent asks clarifying questions (onset, radiation, medication history).
  2. It maps symptoms to SNOMED codes and flags a potential Lisinopril-induced cough.
  3. Chain-of-thought reasoning ranks “acute coronary syndrome” highest and retrieves the latest ACC/AHA guideline.
  4. Treatment plan: immediate ECG & troponin order, discontinuation of Lisinopril pending physician approval, and cardiology referral.

Results & Impact

  • Operational Efficiency: Reduced average triage time from 12 min to 7 min (42 % faster).
  • Enhanced Accuracy: 96 % concordance with senior clinician triage decisions on a 5 k-case test set.
  • High Scalability: Sustains 1 100 concurrent user sessions at < 1.2 s median latency.
  • Clinical Interoperability: 100 % of outputs delivered in SNOMED-CT / ICD-10 for seamless EHR ingestion.
