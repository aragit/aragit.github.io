Production patterns

We classify systems by what must never happen — the failure mode that destroys trust, violates safety, or collapses economic value. Each pattern is a control structure that binds neural generation to deterministic verification.

PATTERN A

Guarded execution

Liability: A wrong action causes irreversible harm.

A deterministic rules engine controls the workflow. Neural models are invoked for specific, bounded fuzzy-logic tasks — but the symbolic engine owns execution, owns veto, and owns fallback. There is no override.

Clinical decision support, autonomous vehicle control, industrial safety, energy grid balancing.

Failure modes we engineer against

  • Neural planner deadlock: Escalate to deterministic fallback + human review
  • Guard bypass attempt: All paths compile to DAG before runtime; no runtime interpreter
  • Stale policy corpus: Versioned, cryptographically signed policies; full re-validation on update
PATTERN B

Verify-then-generate

Liability: A wrong conclusion wastes time, money, or opportunity — but can be corrected.

A neural reasoner proposes a solution. An external ontology or graph verifies it. Violations trigger a self-correction feedback loop with confidence decay. After maximum iterations, control transfers to a deterministic fallback or human.

Scientific discovery, differential diagnosis, research synthesis, speculative design.

Failure modes we engineer against

  • Max self-correction exceeded: Transfer to Guarded Execution fallback (static DAG)
  • Verifier contradiction: Meta-cognitive arbitration weights verifiers by historical accuracy
  • Confidence decay stall: Treat as convergence failure; trigger human handoff
PATTERN C

Algorithmic arbitration

Liability: Multiple autonomous actors compete for shared resources. No single actor can be trusted.

Neural agents propose strategies. A compiled mathematical engine — Nash equilibrium solver, VCG auction mechanism, LP verifier — checks every proposal against formal invariants. No strategy proceeds without mathematical proof.

Ad auctions, procurement negotiation, market mechanisms, resource allocation.

  • Budget invariant violation: Immediate suspension + proportional fairness redistribution
  • Strategy cycle detection: Switch to fictitious play (time-averaged strategies)

What these systems are not

We do not claim academic taxonomy compliance. Our systems are engineering hybrids — modular architectures where neural and symbolic components interact via explicit APIs and interfaces, not unified monolithic weights.

Claim we do not makeWhy
"Native neural-symbolic binding" (Kautz Type 6)Our symbolic verifiers are external systems (Neo4j, OPA, SciPy), not compiled into neural weights
"Logic as differentiable loss" (Kautz Type 5)Our policies are hard constraints (Rego, Cypher), not soft regularization terms
"Neuro-symbolic by post-hoc validation"Post-hoc checking is standard ML, not a structural pattern. Our validation is in-loop or pre-execution.

What we do claim: Production systems with measurable guarantees — property-based tests, benchmark alignment, immutable audit trails, and deterministic escalation.

AXIOMIS

Our clinical intelligence system. A deterministic FHIR state machine at its core, migrating toward more flexible reasoning as validation frameworks mature — with safety invariants that are non-negotiable.

  • Every treatment plan compiles to a DAG before execution.
  • Every node is pre-flight checked by OPA/Rego policies.
  • Every execution trace is committed to an immutable ledger.
  • Every override of a neural recommendation requires explicit symbolic justification.

This is how we cross the Planning Rubicon without crossing the safety line.
