Mentoring, Not Teaching

Courses and tutorials teach you syntax and isolated skills. Mentoring helps you develop technical taste and strategic thinking.

We won't just cover how to implement solutions; we'll debate architectural trade-offs, discuss how to avoid common pitfalls, and work through the real-world problems you're facing.

Who This Is For

  • ML Engineers seeking to transition to a Senior or Staff role.
  • Data Scientists looking to master production-level ML and MLOps.
  • LLM Engineers tackling advanced agentic workflows or fine-tuning challenges.
  • Software Engineers pivoting into a dedicated AI/ML career path.

How It Works: A Collaborative Partnership

Our engagement is structured in 4-week blocks, which can be renewed for ongoing development. Each block is a focused sprint to achieve your specific goals.

1

Goal Setting & Deep Dive

We define what "level-up" means for you and establish clear, ambitious goals. We'll review your current projects, code, and challenges to build a custom curriculum.

2

Collaborative Problem-Solving

This is the core of the mentorship. Our weekly 60-minute sessions focus on hands-on topics: code reviews, architectural design, debugging complex issues, and navigating project strategy.

3

Retrospective & Path Forward

We review your progress against our goals, solidify key learnings, and build a concrete plan for your continued independent growth.

4

Ongoing Support

Between sessions, you'll have asynchronous access via Slack/Discord for quick questions, feedback, and resource sharing.

My Mentoring Philosophy & Experience

My approach is practical, direct, and inspired by effective programs like the KaggleX Mentorship Program, where I've served as a mentor. My goal is not to give you the answers, but to help you develop the framework to find them yourself. We focus on practical application and the unwritten rules of building effective, real-world AI systems.
